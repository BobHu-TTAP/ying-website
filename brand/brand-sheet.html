01 — Logo system

The mark & lockups

A winged “Y” with forked, ascending arms (momentum — 赢, to win / advance) and a split stem, in a two-tone navy + teal. One master mark drives every variant; it reads from favicon to signage.

Primary horizontal lockup — default for headers, letterheads, signatures.

Reversed — on navy, photography or dark backgrounds.

Stacked + 赢 — square / centred placements.

Monogram — avatars, stamps, small spaces.

App icon / favicon — navy tile.

One-colour black — single-ink print, fax, engraving.

Clear space — keep free space on all sides equal to the height of the wing tips (shown dashed).

Minimum sizes

Monogram ≥ 24px

Favicon ≥ 16px

Full lockup ≥ 120px wide

04 — Usage

Do & don’t

  • Use the navy + teal mark on light backgrounds.
  • On navy / photos, use the white + bright-teal reversed mark.
  • Keep clear space ≥ the height of the wing tips on all sides.
  • Set 赢 / 英 in Ma Shan Zheng for display; 中文 body in Noto Sans SC.
  • Outline the wordmark text for print / third-party handoff.
  • Don’t recolour the mark outside the palette.
  • Don’t stretch, rotate, or add effects (shadows, bevels).
  • Don’t swap the navy / teal wings or even them to one colour (except the one-colour and reversed variants).
  • Don’t set teal body text on white (use Teal Ink, or navy).
  • Don’t add yin-yang, dragon or other literal motifs.
